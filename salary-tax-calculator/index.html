Salary Tax Guide for Pakistan

Pakistan Income Tax Slabs FY 2025-2026 (Salaried)

These slabs apply to salaried individuals as defined by the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) for the fiscal year 2025-2026:

  • Up to PKR 600,000: 0% tax
  • PKR 600,001 to 1,200,000: 5% on amount above PKR 600,000
  • PKR 1,200,001 to 2,400,000: PKR 30,000 + 15% on amount above PKR 1,200,000
  • PKR 2,400,001 to 3,600,000: PKR 210,000 + 25% on amount above PKR 2,400,000
  • PKR 3,600,001 to 6,000,000: PKR 510,000 + 30% on amount above PKR 3,600,000
  • Above PKR 6,000,000: PKR 1,230,000 + 35% on amount above PKR 6,000,000

Note: These are basic income tax slabs for salaried individuals. Actual tax may vary with exemptions, rebates, and other adjustments. Always verify with the latest FBR notification.

How Pakistan Salary Tax Works

Pakistan uses a progressive tax system for salaried income. This means higher income brackets are taxed at higher rates. The tax is calculated on your annual gross salary, then divided into monthly deductions by your employer.

Your employer deducts tax monthly under Section 149 of the Income Tax Ordinance 2001. This is called Tax Deducted at Source (TDS). At year-end, your employer provides a tax certificate showing total tax deducted.

What Is Effective Tax Rate?

The effective tax rate is the percentage of your total income that goes to tax. Because Pakistan uses progressive slabs, your effective rate is always lower than the highest slab rate you fall into.

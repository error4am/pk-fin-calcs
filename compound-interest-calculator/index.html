What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest, which is only calculated on the principal, compound interest grows your money faster over time.

Compound Interest Formula

The formula is: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the final amount, P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate, n is the compounding frequency per year, and t is the time in years.

Why Compounding Frequency Matters

  • Monthly compounding earns slightly more than yearly because interest is calculated and added 12 times per year.
  • Quarterly compounding adds interest every 3 months.
  • Yearly compounding calculates interest once per year, giving the lowest total for the same rate.

In Pakistan, bank savings accounts typically compound monthly or quarterly. Fixed deposit accounts (FDIs) usually compound quarterly or at maturity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal amount. Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any accumulated interest. Over time, compound interest grows your money significantly faster.
Most Pakistani banks offer compound interest on savings accounts and fixed deposits. Ask your bank about the compounding frequency for your specific account type.
For the same annual rate, more frequent compounding gives a slightly higher return. However, the difference between monthly and quarterly compounding is small compared to the impact of the interest rate itself.
Start early, choose accounts with higher rates, prefer monthly compounding when available, and reinvest interest earned rather than withdrawing it. Time is the most powerful factor in compound growth.

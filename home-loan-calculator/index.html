How Pakistan Home Loans Work

Pakistani banks typically finance 70-80% of the property value as a home loan. The remaining 20-30% is your down payment. Loan tenures usually range from 5 to 25 years, and interest rates vary from 15% to 22% depending on the bank and your credit profile.

Key Things to Consider

  • Down payment: A higher down payment reduces your loan amount and total interest. Most banks require at least 20-30%.
  • Interest rate: Compare rates from multiple banks. Even a 1% difference can save lakhs in total interest.
  • Loan tenure: Longer tenure means lower EMI but much higher total interest. Find a balance you can afford.
  • Processing fees: Banks charge 1-2% of the loan amount as processing fee. Factor this into your budget.
  • Insurance: Some banks require property insurance or life insurance linked to the loan.

Who Can Get a Home Loan in Pakistan?

Most Pakistani banks require you to be a salaried or self-employed individual with a stable income, aged 21-65, with a minimum income threshold. Government employees and employees of reputable organizations usually get easier approval.

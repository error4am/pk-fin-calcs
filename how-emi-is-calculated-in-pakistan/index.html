How EMI Is Calculated in Pakistan

Learn the simple formula behind monthly loan installments and how loan amount, interest rate, and tenure affect repayment in Pakistan.

Why This Matters

Many borrowers focus on whether a bank approves a loan, but the more important question is whether the monthly installment actually fits the budget. EMI, or Equated Monthly Installment, is the amount you pay every month to repay the loan principal and interest over time.

This guide explains the common reducing balance formula used in Pakistan and gives you a faster way to estimate repayment before you compare banks, tenures, or financing offers.

The EMI Formula

The standard reducing balance formula is:

EMI = P x r x (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

Here, P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the total number of monthly installments.

What Each Part Means

  • Principal: The amount you borrow from the bank or lender.
  • Interest rate: The annual rate converted into a monthly rate for the formula.
  • Tenure: The number of months over which you repay the loan.

What Usually Raises EMI

A larger principal, higher interest rate, or shorter repayment period usually increases the monthly installment. Longer tenures reduce EMI but increase total interest paid over the life of the loan.

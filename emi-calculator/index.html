EMI Guide for Pakistan

What Is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed monthly payment you make to repay a loan over a set period. Each EMI includes both principal repayment and interest. In Pakistan, most home loans, car loans, and personal loans use the reducing balance method.

EMI Formula

The standard EMI formula is: EMI = P x r x (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the number of months. This calculator uses that exact formula so your results match bank calculations.

Factors That Affect Your EMI

  • Loan amount: Higher principal means higher EMI.
  • Interest rate: Pakistani bank rates vary from 15% to 25% depending on the loan type and your credit profile.
  • Tenure: Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid.
  • Fixed vs floating rate: Some loans have variable rates that change over time.
